# What is "Play"
**Approach 1**
- free activity
- outside "ordinary" life, "not serious", but absorbing player
- activity connected with no material interest
- proceeds within its own proper boundaries of time & space
- according to fixed rules
- <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">promotes formation of social groups</mark>

**Approach 2** -> more focused on functionality
- play as progress: children's play, development and not entertainment
- as fate: gambling

## On Genres

**Critique**
- not "timeless", formal criteria missing
- tree-based classifications (taxonomies) too rigid (-> mixed genres)
	- graph based required -> can get complicated
- mostly driven from existing games (extensional), not intensional

**Better**
- genre classifications based on <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">game mechanics</mark> 
	- by shared _collection/set_ of core mechanisms
	- additive: new mechanics can be added w.o. changing older parts
	- easy creation of new genres
	- more "timeless"
- derive from single pioneer game and its mechanics and following similar games
